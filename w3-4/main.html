What are calories and why count them?

-What are calories?
Calories are units of energy contained in food that our body can either use immediately or store in reserve.
-How are food calories measured?
Today, products are not heated, but burned in a special chamber, measuring the amount of energy they release using a device - a calorimeter. But even more often they are chemically decomposed into proteins, fats and carbohydrates, their specific gravity and total caloric content are considered.

The number of calories in vegetables

Vegetables contain many useful and nutritious substances necessary for our body, as well as in order to keep ourselves in good shape. They are useful for many reasons, because they can be found in a lot of vitamins and mineral components. Let's look at the some vegetable calorie table!

Food Serving Calories

Pumpkin 1 pumpkin (196 g) 51 cal

Turnips 1 turnip (122 g) 34 cal
Cabbage 1 head (908 g) 227 cal

Useful information

Vegetables are the source of many diseases, for example, the consumption of acids, dyes, roughage and other beneficial compounds with bactericidal properties. Vitamins such as beta-carotene or vitamin C found in fruits and vegetables are effective antioxidants.Since vegetables contain a lot of water and few energy-rich components, they contain few calories and are stored for a very short time. But there are exceptions, such as potatoes and legumes. Thanks to starch, their calorie content is much higher than in other vegetables. Carrots, beets, sweet green peas and cabbage contain sucrose and monosaccharides. It should be remembered that old vegetables already have too much dietary fiber and are therefore not suitable for juices and salads. If you eat vegetables at least 5 times a day, then your body will be more resistant to various diseases. In addition, it has a positive effect on overall well-being. Unfortunately, the human body cannot accumulate useful vitamins and other elements. As an example, consider vitamin C. It is found in many natural foods and is well absorbed. To maintain the required level of vitamin C in the body, one must constantly take care of the use of foods containing this vitamin, such as peppers, broccoli, spinach, parsley, dill, Brussels sprouts, etc. Almost all vegetables need to be stored in the refrigerator. The exception is unripe vegetables, because at room temperature they will become a little more ripe. Also, long-term storage products, such as potatoes and beets, are not required to be stored in the refrigerator, but it is desirable. The most comfortable place in the refrigerator for vegetables is the bottom drawer. Many of them can be safely stored there for up to 1 month. But most vegetables are better to use during the week. If you freeze or buy frozen vegetables, then be calm, because they remain tasty and healthy for a very long time – up to one year. Canned vegetables should be stored in a cool dark place, the optimal temperature should be below 15 degrees.

Fruits have little energy value and a lot of water, so it is advisable to consume them at least 5 times a day. Since different vegetables contain different vitamins, you need to make sure that you have all the necessary fruits in your diet. Even though the energy value of fruits is quite low, excessive consumption of some of them can have an unimportant effect on health.
